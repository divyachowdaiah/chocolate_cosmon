Chocolate Cosmos

Native to Mexico, the Chocolate Cosmos is cherished for its deep maroon petals and sweet chocolate-like scent. It blooms in late summer and is usually grown from tubers since it's extinct in the wild.

  • Botanical Name: Cosmos atrosanguineus
  • Bloom Time: Late Summer
  • Height: Up to 60 cm
  • Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade
  • Water: Moderate; well-drained soil
  • Color: Deep maroon (almost black)
  • Fragrance: Chocolate-like aroma
  • Climate: Warm, frost-free
  • Status: Extinct in wild, garden grown
